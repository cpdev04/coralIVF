What is IVF and how it works?

IVF treatment is a multi-step process that involves several stages, including ovarian stimulation, egg retrieval, fertilization, embryo culture, and embryo transfer. The process is carefully monitored by our team of experienced fertility specialists, who work closely with patients to customize a treatment plan that meets their unique needs and maximizes their chances of success.

IVF or In Vitro Fertilization is one of the more widely known types of Assisted Reproductive Techniques (ART). It involves retrieving eggs from the woman's ovaries and fertilizing them with sperm in a laboratory dish. The resulting embryos are then transferred back into the woman's uterus in the hope of achieving a successful pregnancy. We offer state-of-the-art facilities and a team of experienced doctors and embryologists who work together to provide personalized care and support to couples undergoing IVF. With the help of IVF, couples struggling with infertility can have a chance to conceive and start a family.

Here's How It Works :

Ovulation Induction

This step involves stimulating the ovaries to produce multiple eggs.

Egg Retrieval

Eggs are retrieved from the woman's ovaries using a minimally invasive procedure.

Sperm Collection

Sperm sample is collected from the male partner or a sperm donor.

Fertilization

Eggs and sperm are combined in a laboratory dish to facilitate fertilization.

Embryo Culture

Fertilized embryos are cultured in a controlled environment to promote development.

Embryo Transfer

One or more embryos are transferred into the woman's uterus.

Pregnancy Test

A pregnancy test is performed to determine if the IVF procedure was successful.

When is IVF Recommended?

It is important to note that IVF is a complex and expensive procedure, and it is not always successful.
It is important to discuss all of the potential risks and benefits with a fertility specialist before deciding whether or not to pursue IVF.

Some common reasons for recommending IVF include:

  • Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
  • Male fertility problems
  • Unexplained infertility
  • Ovulation disorders
  • Endometriosis
  • Advanced maternal age

Factors Affecting the Success Rate of an IVF Cycle

Overall, the success rate of IVF can vary greatly depending on these and other factors. It is important for patients to discuss their specific situation with their healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment.

There are several factors that can affect the success rate of an in vitro fertilization (IVF) cycle. These include:

  • Age
  • Fertility diagnosis
  • Quality of the eggs and sperm
  • Number of embryos transferred
  • Lifestyle factors
  • Embryo quality
